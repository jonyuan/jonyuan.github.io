Taiwanese American Student Association (TASA)

On campus I spend most of my time working with the TASA board to host cool campus-wide events, including the annual Spring Lantern Festival, speaker event (we have previously featured Anna Akana, The Jubilee Project, Steven Lim, and Elephante), seasonal Food Workshops, and (for the first time this year) a Taiwanese Night Market.

Most notably for me was winning the William J. Michel RSO of the Year Award in May 2019. We work hard to host fun and inclusive cultural events for everyone on campus, and I am excited for what's to come for the club in the coming years.

I am currently working on developing the organization's website.


Quantitative Trading Strategies

I work with an MC alumnus, Joe Fennessey, to test new strategies. In the fall of 2018 I picked up working on trading strategies as an interest, and use pandas/numpy to modify and implement commodity and volatility futures strategies for backtesting under Joe's framework. The strategies I have worked on are entirely algorithmic.

In the past year, I specialized on volatility strategies using VIX futures and have implemented around a dozen algorithms, utilizing momentum, term-structure, volatility risk premium, a GARCH model, and other indicators. My best performing strategy utilizes daily future roll returns and has a significant positive Sharpe.

I still work on testing new strategies, and would be happy to discuss my work.
